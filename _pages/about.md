## Junghyun Shin (신중현)

AI Engineer & Reinforcement Learning Researcher based in Seoul, Korea.

I build robots that move like animals — starting from quadrupeds, towards humanoids.

---

### Research Interests

- **RL-based Locomotion Control** — PPO, SAC, asymmetric Actor-Critic for legged robots
- **Sim-to-Real Adaptation** — Residual Neural Networks, system identification, PACE/CMA-ES
- **Natural Motion** — Adversarial Motion Priors (AMP), Deep Mimic, mammal-like gait generation
- **Perception + Control Pipeline** — Integrating vision (detection, segmentation) into locomotion

---

### Background

**Physics → Computer Vision → RL Robotics**

I studied physics at Hanyang University, which gave me a strong foundation in analytical mechanics, linear algebra, and statistics. That background shapes how I approach robotics — not as a tuning exercise, but as a problem of understanding the underlying dynamics.

| Period | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| 2022–2024 | Vision: object detection, segmentation, model optimization |
| 2025–present | RL locomotion: quadruped sim-to-real, C++ deployment, natural motion |

**Learning**

- PyTorch, custom reward engineering, domain randomization

**Deployment**

---

### Long-term Vision

> "Robots that traverse sparse terrain like a mountain goat — jumping point to point, stabilizing mid-air, integrating perception seamlessly — and eventually humanoids doing the same."

The roadmap:

1. Robust flat-ground + unstructured terrain walking (quadruped)
2. Sparse foothold navigation using perception pipeline
3. Mammal-like natural motion via reference trajectory learning
4. Humanoid extension + embodied reasoning (ER models)
